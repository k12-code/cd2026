Framework

Objectives

This analysis is structured around two distinct but connected goals. Every category section answers both.

Objective 1 — Does buying by Dx create net positive retention?

Does buying this category by D0/7/14/21/28 result in a buyer retention rate meaningfully above non-buyers?
To report both the absolute buyer retention % and non-buyer retention % — not just the gap — to see whether a changing delta reflects buyer improvement, non-buyer improvement, or both moving together.
Signed delta (buyer minus non-buyer) is used throughout. Negative delta = buyers retain worse than non-buyers at that window.

Objective 2 — How has this delta changed, and why?

Points of inflection: identified as a ≥5% sustained shift in buyer %, buyer ret, non-buyer ret, or delta that held for ≥2 weeks.
For each inflection: what changed in pricing (MRP/SP), discount %, SKU count, and customer volumes — and which direction.
Explicit call-out of whether a delta change was driven by buyer ret dropping, non-buyer ret rising, or both.
Last 4 weeks assessed vs historical baseline — whether current metrics are above, at, or below the sustained period average.

02 — Coconut Water (CCW)

CCW: Seasonality drop at Sep 15, slow D28 recovery

Objective 1 — Does buying CCW create positive retention?

D7 window: No. Average signed delta at D7 = −1.55pp. CCW buyers at D7 retain worse than non-buyers (buyer avg 19.6% vs non-buyer 21.2%). Buying CCW early is not a retention signal.
D28 window: Yes, and improving. D28 signed delta went from −2.83pp (early period, Aug–Sep) to +3.18pp (late period, Dec–Jan). CCW needs ~28 days of engagement before the buyer quality becomes visible.
Conclusion: Do not use CCW as an early (D7) trigger. It is the only category where the D28 signal is improving. Use it only as a D21–D28 supplementary indicator.

Objective 2 — Delta change & reasons

Key inflection: Sep 15. Buyer share (D7) fell from 2.6% (Aug–Sep) to ~1.1% (Dec–Jan) — an absolute decline of 1.5pp, a relative fall of ~58%.
Delta at D7 also compressed: early period −5.6pp → late period +1.0pp. This looks like recovery but is driven by fewer, more-committed CCW buyers remaining in the pool.
Why: No SKU change. SP rose +3% (discount −3pp). Pure demand contraction — consistent with end-of-summer seasonal cooling. Buyer share never recovered.

Seasonality validation needed: If this is seasonal, CCW buyer share should recover from March 2026 as summer demand returns. If buyer share stays at ~1% in April–May, the drop is structural.

03 — Ghee | Touchpoint 1

Ghee Oct 10: Post-festive demand cool-down

Objective 1 — Does buying Ghee by D7/D28 create positive retention?

D7 window: Marginally, then negative. D7 signed delta averaged +0.63pp for the period — barely positive. By the late period (Dec–Jan), it turned negative (−1.18pp late 30-day avg). Ghee buyers at D7 now retain slightly worse than non-buyers.
D28 window: Was strong, now collapsed. D28 MA28 delta fell from 8.38% (Aug) to 2.37% (Jan) — a 5.9pp absolute compression. This is the steepest delta collapse of any category.
D0 window: Now deteriorating. D0 MA7 went from −0.50% (Aug) to −3.83% (Jan) — getting more negative, not less. First-order ghee buyers are not high retainers.

Objective 2 — Two inflections, two different causes

Oct 10: Pure demand drop. −9.8% customers. No pricing change. Core Desi Danedar 850ml lost 4,863 customers (−16.1% relative). Post-festive cooldown. Not offer-driven.
Nov 21: Counter-intuitive. Customers UP +10% but delta collapsed. Combo push: 850ml x2 attracted 12,768 new customers at SP −₹82. More buyers, but lower-quality ones → retention drops.
The delta drop at D28 is driven by buyer retention falling (from ~25.5% early to ~23.5% late), while non-buyer retention was broadly stable (~20–21%). The widening combo use is diluting the buyer pool quality.

The paradox: Customers grew +10% (from 48,309 to 53,132 — an absolute increase of 4,823 users). Orders grew +11.8%. Yet the D28 retention delta collapsed from ~5.3% to ~2%. This is the clearest evidence in the dataset that volume ≠ quality.
The culprit: Desi Danedar Ghee 850ml x2 combo went from 7,205 customers (pre) to 19,973 customers (post) — an increase of +12,768 customers (+177% relative). SP fell from ₹1,314 to ₹1,232 — a drop of ₹82 (−6.2% relative, discount increased +4.6pp absolutely). This single SKU drove all the growth and all the quality deterioration.
Why delta collapsed — the mechanics: The combo attracted price-sensitive bulk buyers who were not organically motivated to buy ghee. These users' D60–90 platform retention is lower, pulling buyer retention down. Meanwhile non-buyer retention was stable at ~20–21%. The delta narrowed because buyer retention fell, not because non-buyer retention improved.
The same pattern repeated Nov 28–Dec 11: 850ml x2 combo gained another +9,854 customers (from 13,729 to 23,583), SP −₹78. This is now a structural pattern, not a one-off. Every time the combo runs, the buyer pool quality degrades and delta falls the following period.
Action: Stop running Desi Danedar 850ml x2 combo for users ≤D7. Let first-time buyers discover ghee through the single-unit 850ml. The retention data clearly shows single-unit buyers have higher quality.

05 — Fruits & Vegetables (FnV)

FnV: Offer removal on daily items caused −17% customer drop

Objective 1 — Does buying FnV by D7 create positive retention?

D7: Near-zero positive. Avg signed delta = +0.25pp. Effectively neutral — FnV at D7 is not a meaningful predictor of D60–90 retention.
D28: Meaningful and stable. D28 avg delta = +3.92pp, early 4.71pp → late 3.44pp. A genuine positive signal at 4 weeks — users who keep buying FnV for a month are better retained.
Verdict: FnV is a frequency/habit category. Do not use D7 as an evaluation window. D21+ is when the signal materialises. The Oct 9 drop compressed buyer share (from ~5.3% to ~4.3% at D7) without materially changing the quality of the remaining buyers.

Objective 2 — Oct 9 inflection

Buyer share at D7 fell from ~5.3% (pre-Oct 9) to ~4.3% (post-Oct 9) — an absolute fall of ~1pp, relative decline of ~19%. This was sustained for the rest of the period.
Delta itself barely moved at D7 (was always near-zero). But the D28 delta showed mild compression (early 4.71pp → late 3.44pp), likely related to cohort mix changing post-Oct.
Root cause: SP went up on all top items. Tomato +₹3, Potato +₹1, Bhindi +₹1, Coriander +₹6. This is subsidised pricing being removed. Discount fell −4.1pp absolute across FnV.

08 — Staples

Staples Dec 1: Toor Dal price hike drove pulse abandonment

Objective 1 — Does buying Staples by D7 create positive retention?

D7: No. Signed delta at D7 = −0.68pp. Staples buyers in their first 7 days retain slightly worse than non-buyers (20.4% vs 21.1%).
D28: Yes, and improving. D28 avg delta = +4.98pp (early 4.94pp, late 5.32pp — actually improving). MA7 ends at 5.70% vs starting at 3.40%.
Verdict: Strong late signal, negative early signal. Classic slow-burn category. Any D7-based trigger on Staples will generate false positives (flagging buyers who underperform baseline).

Objective 2 — Dec 1 inflection

Buyer share at D7 fell from ~2.9% (early period) to ~2.3% (late period) — −0.6pp absolute, −21% relative.
Buyer ret and non-buyer ret both declined slightly in late period — but buyer ret fell more, so the D7 delta became more negative. The signal was already negative and worsened.
Root cause: Toor Dal 450gm SP +₹19 (+31% relative). Lost 2,539 customers (−54.6% relative). This is a commodity price pass-through that hit a high-frequency purchase item for new users.

CCW Rebound: Self-selection, not platform action

The D28 delta for CCW improved from −2.83pp (early period) to +3.18pp (late period). The question: was this driven by something the platform did?

These are two separate problems requiring two separate actions: (1) Sustaining D28 signal quality → no action needed, self-maintaining. (2) Recovering CCW buyer share in new cohorts → requires active subsidisation or placement.

FnV D60-90: Platform retention declining, re-buy rate consistently low

This answers two questions he asked: (1) Do FnV D28 buyers retain on the platform? (2) Do they re-buy FnV? Both in absolute count and %. Data covers cohorts Oct 29 2025 – Feb 24 2026 (the window where D60-90 has completed).

Yes — but it's declining. Nov 1–14: 30.0% of FnV D28 buyers were retained at D60-90 (20,498 out of 68,422). Feb 1–24: only 20.6% retained (15,730 out of 76,308).

Key insight: FnV is not a habit-forming category. Users who buy FnV in their first 28 days are better platform retainers — but not because they became FnV re-buyers. FnV buying is a proxy for platform engagement, not an FnV loyalty signal.
