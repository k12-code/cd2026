Design Rationale · All 4 Screens
Why each screen is built the way it is

D0 Homepage — offer-first, not category-first

The hero leads with "Your first 4 days of milk, on us" — anchoring intent before product. Three key details (7 AM delivery, 4+4 deal, zero risk) are scannable at a glance. The 4+4 teaser card below the fold keeps the offer visible throughout browsing without overwhelming. CTA copy mirrors the offer language to reduce decision friction.

Milk PDP — price block above lab report, trial framing as primary CTA

Price and the free-trial note appear before the quality report — users scan price first. The lab card moves below the CTA as validation, not gatekeeping. "7 AM", "4 AM tested", and "4+4" float in the hero as scannable delivery facts, visible before any scroll.

Post-order — 7 AM highlighted in timeline, trial reflected in order summary

The farm-to-door timeline now highlights "Your door · by 7 AM" in gold — the delivery promise becomes the emotional payoff. The order summary relabels the discount line as "4-day free trial applied" to reinforce the offer frame. Dairy cross-sell uses trust transfer: same farm, same milk.

Notifications — consistent offer language across all pushes

D2 now includes "arrives by 7 AM" in the body. D5 restates "Your first 4 days are on us" — the same offer headline from the homepage, so it feels like a reminder rather than a new pitch. Sequencing remains intent-driven: urgency → trust → habit → social proof.
