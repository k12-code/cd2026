02 — Coconut Water

CCW

Does buying CCW by D7 create positive retention?

D7: No. Avg signed delta = −1.55pp. CCW buyers at D7 retain worse than non-buyers (19.6% vs 21.2%). D7 CCW buying is not a retention signal.

D28: Yes, and improving. D28 signed delta went from −2.83pp (early) to +3.18pp (late). CCW needs ~28 days before the buyer quality becomes visible.

Is the Sep 15 drop seasonal?

Disproved. Mar 2026 ≤D7 customers are 37.6% below Sep 2025 despite both being peak summer. New-user discount was −15pp lower in March vs September — the platform suppressed recovery by cutting new-user discounts in the season they were needed most.

Country CW 1pc: 8,498 (Sep 2025) → 5,547 (Mar 2026) = −34.7% for new users.

D28 delta rebound: Customers fell −24.1% (Oct→Nov) with zero platform action. The improving quality signal is cohort self-selection — only the most committed CCW buyers remained. Buyer share never recovered. Quality and reach are moving in opposite directions and need separate interventions.

05 — Ghee

Ghee: Combo promotions are destroying buyer quality

Confirmed pattern across two consecutive events. Every time the 850ml x2 combo runs, ≤D7 buyer count surges, SP drops sharply, and D28 retention delta collapses 4–8 weeks later. This is no longer a one-off — it is a structural mechanism.

Nov 21 combo push

≤D7 customers: +8.8% (+671)

SP: −₹65.6 (−7.1%)

D28 delta result: 5.3% → ~2% collapse

Buyer ret fell, non-buyer stable → delta narrowed because buyer quality degraded.

Nov 28 combo push

≤D7 customers: +27.0% (+2,215)

SP: −₹79.8 (−9.6%)

Pull even stronger for new users (+27% vs +17.7% all-user)

Combo disproportionately targets/attracts new users via deep discount.

Oct 10 drop (−4.9%) was separate — post-festive demand cooldown. No pricing action, no combo. SP rose +₹40.7 for new users though — investigate whether new-user Ghee pricing tier is intentionally higher.

06 — Snacks

Snacks: Highest signal, most vulnerable to offer dilution

Objective 1 — Does buying Snacks create positive retention?

Yes — highest of any category at D28. D28 delta = +11.9pp (buyer 32.3% vs non-buyer 20.4%). D7 delta = +5.83pp avg, compressed to +3.8pp late period.

Buyer share is tiny: 0.8% at D7 (1,729 ≤D7 buyers). Confirmed via pool size — not a signal failure but a reach problem.

Delta narrowing

Post-Nov 28: Buyer ret fell −4.3pp (28.5%→24.3%). Non-buyer ret fell −2.1pp (21.6%→19.5%). Buyer fell more → delta narrowed.

The offer attracted lower-retention buyers, diluting the enthusiast pool.

07 — Staples

Staples: Negative D7 signal, strong D28 signal

Should not use Staples at D7 as an activation trigger. D7 delta is negative throughout (−0.67pp early → −0.35pp late) — Staples buyers in their first 7 days retain slightly worse than non-buyers. The signal only becomes meaningful at D21+ (delta climbs to +5.0pp at D28, improving over time).

Dec 1 Toor Dal price hike (SP +₹19, +31%): both buyer ret (−0.86pp) and non-buyer ret (−1.41pp) declined. Non-buyer fell more → signed delta improved slightly. This was a broad cohort quality decline, not Staples-buyer-specific. Do not over-attribute to Staples action.

08 — Milk

Milk: Loyalty intact, funnel narrowing

Per-buyer quality: stable

Buyer retention held at 21.7% throughout the period — the quality of Milk buyers has not degraded.

D7 delta compressed from 7.54pp (early) to 3.25pp (late) — but this is driven by non-buyer baseline fluctuation, not buyer quality decline.

Buyer share: declining

D7 buyer share fell from 18.2% (Aug 2025) to 15.4% (Jan 2026) — a 2.8pp absolute decline, 15% relative fall.

114,019 ≤D7 Milk buyers — still the dominant early category. But fewer new users are making their first Milk purchase in Week 1.

The HP Milk surge post-Sep 15 (HP Cow Milk +10K customers, HP Paneer +3,187 at SP −₹13) is offer-driven. If offer is removed, adoption may not sustain. Track D60-90 retention of HP Milk buyers specifically vs core Milk buyers to understand if the protein positioning is attracting a higher or lower quality cohort.
