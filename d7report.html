Note

How to read
this report

This explains the measurements, the chart anatomy, and when to scrutinise a reading.

The Four Metrics
Buyer Share %
D60–90 retained cat buyers ÷ cohort size
Of all users who joined on a given day, what fraction bought the category within D7 and were still retained at D60–90. Measures how many onboarded users became durable category buyers in their first week.
Delta (pp)
buyer_ret_D60–90 − non_buyer_ret_D60–90
The retention advantage (in percentage points) of users who bought the category in D7 vs. those who didn't. Measures the category's contribution to platform loyalty. Positive = buyers retain better. Negative = buyers churn faster.
Inflection
local_peak or local_trough on raw series
A point where the raw daily series reverses direction by a meaningful amount — signalling a genuine shift in buyer composition or category dynamics. Dots appear on the raw line, not the MA7 smoothing. Green dot = peak. Red dot = trough.
Contribution
Buyer Share × Delta
The combined impact of a category on cohort retention — accounts for both how many users it touches (BS) and how well those users retain (delta). A category with high delta but low BS has low contribution, and vice versa.
Chart Anatomy — Annotated Example (Milk, BS)
Each chart shows the raw daily series (faint line) and a 7-day centred moving average (bold line). Inflection dots are placed on the raw line — the exact day the reversal occurred. The MA7 line shows the underlying trend. Delta charts include a zero reference line: values above zero mean buyers retain better than non-buyers; values below mean the reverse.

What This Tells You
On Sep 01, 23.7% of that day's onboarded users bought Milk within D7 and were retained at D60–90 — up 5pp from the prior trough. The ₹2 price raise on the two main SKUs filtered out low-intent buyers, leaving a pool of committed purchasers who were more likely to be retained long-term. The green dot on the chart marks this exact day on the raw series.
!
When to scrutinise a reading: Snacks daily delta swings of 18pp in a single day are statistically marginal — with 0.6% buyer share, 3–4 buyers determine the entire delta. Use the 7d MA for Snacks. Similarly, the Oct 20 Milk BS trough (16.4%) coincides with the period's smallest cohort day (2,541 users) — verify against absolute buyer counts, not just percentages. Any reading where buyer share < 1% or absolute buyers < 50 should be read as directional, not precise.
How to Read Combined Movements
Buyer Share ↓ & Delta ↓
Fewer users entering the retained buyer pool, and those who do are lower quality. Usually: price hikes that block trial, poor discovery, or weak assortment.
Buyer Share ↑ & Delta ↓
More users entering the category, but lower-intent ones who don't retain. Usually: aggressive discounts or combos that attract trial without commitment.
Buyer Share ↓ & Delta ↑
Fewer users buying, but the remaining ones are stronger. Casual users filtered out, loyal core remains. CCW in winter is a clear example of this seasonal filtering.
Buyer Share ↑ & Delta ↑
Ideal: more users entering and retaining well. Usually driven by a strong hero SKU, good pricing, or a genuinely habit-forming category. Rare in this dataset.

Key Findings

What the data
tells us to do

23 inflection findings across 7 categories, grouped and condensed. The cross-category summary follows.

Milk · Pricing Selection
Delta is a pricing selection proxy — raise prices, delta rises; discount, delta falls
Sep 01 (+10.3pp), Oct 07 (+9.0pp), Dec 01 (+7.4pp) — all three Milk delta peaks followed simultaneous price increases on Buffalo + Cow 450ml. The pattern is structural, not coincidental. The Dec 20 trough (0.5pp) followed the broadest discount wave of the period.
Milk · Structural Decline
Buyer share fell from 23.7% to 13.4% — cohort quality decline, not pricing
The BS decline is a separate signal from the delta pattern. It reflects the shift in cohort composition over the period — later cohorts are less likely to be milk-first users. This is a platform-level acquisition story, not a Milk category story.
Milk Products · Seasonal Selection
Nov 22 peak (10.4pp) driven by seasonal self-selection, not a product launch
Ghar Jaisa Dahi and Low Fat Dahi were active since Aug 13 — not new launches. As Chaach/Lassi orders fell from 125/day in Sep to 69/day by Nov 22, summer casual buyers exited and loyal year-round MP users remained. This self-selection is what lifted delta. The Oct 29 peak (10.2pp) was pricing-driven (festive hikes).
Milk Products · BS Halved
Buyer share halved from 6.6% to 3.7% — driven by paneer/dahi price hikes
The Oct 16 trough (4.7%) followed a coordinated 3-SKU raise (Taaza Paneer ₹4, Ghar Jaisa Dahi ₹4, HP Paneer ₹6). Despite strong delta, fewer users are trying the category in D7. Entry-point pricing is a concern.
Ghee · Structural Collapse
Delta went from +8.8pp to −5.1pp — driven by 400ml pricing instability
The 400ml entry SKU (MRP ₹530) saw SP swings of ₹82–₹259 across the period. On Jan 06, it had only 35 orders — its lowest single-day count — driving the delta trough. The Combo 850ml×2 was renamed (not discontinued) in Nov and continued trading. Pricing volatility on the entry SKU is the primary cause.
Ghee · Entry SKU Volatility
Stabilise 400ml pricing — erratic SP deters first-time buyers and suppresses delta
The Jan 06 trough (−5.1pp) followed the 400ml hitting ₹171 with only 35 orders. The Sep 17 peak (8.8pp) — driven by ₹6 cut on 850ml + ₹29 cut on 400ml — shows the signal is strong when entry pricing is right. Unlike Milk, Ghee delta improves on discounts, not hikes.
Snacks · Highest Delta
16.1pp on Nov 22 — highest delta of any category in the entire period
No SKU driver — the Nov 22 cohort was unusually exploration-driven. The signal recurs (Sep 01: 13.3pp, Oct 23: 13.2pp, Dec 29: 13.3pp), confirming Snacks buyers are reliably high-retention users. The reach problem is structural.
Snacks · Reach Gap
0.8% buyer share means the delta signal barely touches cohort retention
At 0.8% BS and 6.1pp avg delta, Snacks contributes ~0.05pp to cohort retention. At 2% BS, contribution would rival Milk's total impact. Sampling, add-on prompts, or onboarding nudges are the lever — not pricing (no SKU-driven inflections in the period).
CCW · Worst Delta
−9.5pp on Sep 02 — caused by a pricing decision at the worst possible time
Country CW 1pc SP rose from ₹54 to ₹63 (+₹9) and Combo from ₹101 to ₹120 (+₹19) between Aug 27–Sep 02 — applied at the one window when CCW had peak volume. The Aug–Sep window is when CCW has the most buyers and the most opportunity to lock in loyal users. Raising prices then directly cost the platform its best CCW retention window.
CCW · Survivor Selection
Dec 29: +5.8pp delta — the winter buyer is a different, more loyal profile
Despite buyer share falling 75% Aug→Dec, the winter delta rose to +5.8pp (best in the category's period). CCW's uptrend in winter is survivor selection, not improvement — those who still buy in the off-season are year-round loyalists, disproportionately valuable to platform retention.
FnV · Catalog Proxy
Buyer share is an SKU availability metric — not a demand signal
Sep 04 peak: Okra 250gm (824 ord, SP ₹2) + Potato 500gm (720 ord, SP ₹2) — existing SKUs at deep discount, not new launches. Oct 22 peak: same two SKUs (559 + 453 orders, SP ₹2). Potato 450gm and Okra 225gm launched Nov 08, not Oct — they are pack-size renames of the same vegetables. Treating renames as new products creates artificial inflections.
FnV · Wrong Window
D7 FnV delta averages ~0 — real signal only emerges at D28 (+3.9pp)
The Dec 03 trough (−3.2pp) coincides with winter vegetable discounts (Carrot + Cauliflower ₹7–11), confirming that price-driven FnV trial does not produce retained users. This mirrors Staples at D7: the retention signal takes longer to manifest.
Staples · Wrong Window
D7 is the wrong window to judge Staples — D28 delta is +5pp
Staples D7 delta is persistently negative (avg −0.7pp) — buyers who try Staples in week one appear to be low-intent one-time purchasers at D7. But at D28, delta is +5pp. The category's retention signal takes longer to manifest than the D7 measurement window captures.
Staples · Single Lever
Sugar 900gm pricing is the most reliable single BS lever in the portfolio
The Oct 27 period high (4.3% BS) coincided with Poha Thick 450gm at ₹20.3 and Sugar 900gm at ₹12.1 (avg 291 ord/day). Original claim of ₹3 Sugar cut + ₹10 Poha cut was overstated — actual SP movements were ~₹1 each. Sugar 900gm remains the most correlated BS lever (avg 314 ord/day), but the Oct 27 peak was likely driven more by demand conditions than a specific discount.
